The following timeline shows famous
designers of different decades with
some description about them and some
of their famous works.

Charles Frederick Worth

He is british fashion designer who founded House Of Worth which is a French house of high fashion that specializes in haute couture, ready-to-wear clothes, and perfumes. A famous fashion houses in the 18th, 19th and early 20th centuries. Worth was considered to be one of the main people involved in revolutionising the business of fashion. He was also the lead fashion designer for Empress Eugenie (Empress Consort of the French).

FAMOUS CREATIONS: 1865 ball gown, Green velvet and silver moiré court dress and wedding dresses

Gabrielle ‘Coco’ Chanel

Coco Chanel a french fashion designer and a business was one of the biggest names in the fashion. After being unsuccessful in finding work as a singer, she finally went into the fashion industry by opening a boutique called Chanel in Paris. Chanel was credited with liberating women from the constraints of the "corseted silhouette" and popularizing a sporty, casual chic as the feminine standard of style. She revolutionised the way women wore clothes, paving a new way for the fashion brand. Her simple but elegant styles changed the course of fashion history.

FAMOUS CREATIONS: little black dress(LBD), trousers and suits for women.

Elsa Schiaparelli

Elsa Schiaparelli an Italian fashion designer was one of the world's leading fashion designers in the 1920s. After working at a boutique in New York, Schiaparelli moved to Paris, where she began designing her own clothes. Her work and sense of style shaped the look of fashion in the 1920s and her clothes were worn by some of the world's most famous women, including American actress Greta Garbo.

FAMOUS CREATIONS: The Circus Collection, Evening ensemble and strapless evening dresses.

Bonnie Cashin

Bonnie Cashin an American designer is considered one of the pioneering designers of American sportswear. After the U.S. entered World War II, Cashin designed uniforms for women in the armed forces. She created practical, uncomplicated clothing that catered to the independent woman of the post-war era. in 1952, she opened her own business Bonnie Cashin Designs. Cashin was the first designer chosen for Patterns of The Times an American Designer Series.

FAMOUS CREATIONS: Ensemble comprising trousers and blouse in cadmium orange wool jersey, Suit ensemble comprising bolero jacket and skirt, Jumpsuit of black leather with hood.

Bruce Papas

Bruce Papas a New Zealand fashion designer was known for his fabrics, design, drafting, pattern making, hand cording and embroidery. He started his own business named Staevros Gowns. Bruce continued to specialise in haute couture for a small number of clients. His wedding and bridesmaids gowns were very famous. A large collection of his garments and design sketches are held at the Auckland War Memorial Museum.

FAMOUS CREATIONS: Golden Peacock gown and bespoke gowns

Joan Talbot

Joan Talbot is New Zealand fashion designer born in Sydney, Australia. She started work at Tarantella which was owned by the Tarrant family.Later took over ownership of the business, Joan continued the Tarantella label, as well as introducing a new label Caprice. By the mid 1960s, she was designing two ranges a year for the wholesale market. She has won many awards including Silver and Golden Shears Awards and Wool Board Fashion Choice awards.

FAMOUS CREATIONS: Black and white poncho outfit, wool gown and cape with plisse pleats

Elisabeth Findlay

Elisabeth Findlay is a New Zealand fashion designer born in Greece. She started of her career by working in a clothing company in Auckland and moved on to open her Tart boutique store in Auckland. Later she co-founded the fashion house Zambesi with her husband, Neville Findlay. Zambesi is one of the most iconic and longest-running labels in New Zealand. Known for intelligent, unexpected use of fabric and layers.

FAMOUS CREATIONS: Edge of Darkness collections and uniforms for Air New Zealand.
